<p><p>The high-temperature lubricant and grease market is categorized primarily into soap thickener and non-soap thickener types. Soap thickeners, commonly made from lithium, calcium, or aluminum soaps, offer excellent high-temperature stability and water resistance, making them suitable for various industrial applications. Non-soap thickeners, such as polyurea and clay, provide enhanced thermal stability and performance under extreme conditions, often preferred in specialized applications requiring low volatility and reduced friction. Both types are essential for ensuring optimal equipment performance in high-temperature environments.</p></p>
